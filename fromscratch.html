Basic History

Golf is one of the oldest sports in recorded history, with roots tracing back to 15th-century Scotland. The game has evolved from a pastime of Scottish shepherds hitting pebbles into holes with sticks, into a globally revered sport steeped in tradition, honor, and gentlemanly conduct. Today it is played by over 60 million people across more than 200 countries.

How to play

Golf is played over 18 holes. Each hole has a designated par or the number of strokes (hits) an expert golfer is expected to take. The player with the fewest total strokes wins.

  1. Set Up Your Shot
    • Select the appropriate club (driver, iron, wedge, or putter)
    • Read the wind and terrain
    • Position your feet shoulder-width apart
  2. The Swing
    • Keep your head down and eye on the ball
    • Rotate your hips, not just your arms
    • Follow through completely — don't stop at impact
  3. Approach the Green
    • Use shorter irons and wedges for precision
    • Account for elevation change and bunkers
    • Land short and let the ball roll to the pin
  4. Putting
    • Read the slope of the green
    • Keep a smooth, pendulum-like stroke
    • Aim for a consistent pace, not just direction

Scoring Terms

  • Albatross: 3 under par
  • Eagle: 2 under par
  • Birdie: 1 under par
  • Par: Expected score
  • Bogey: 1 over par
  • Double Bogey: 2 over par

Todays Famous Tournaments

"The 4 Majors"

The Masters

Augusta, Georgia, USA — Held every April at Augusta National Golf Club, The Masters is the most prestigious tournament in golf. Known for its iconic Amen Corner, azalea-lined fairways, and the iconic green jacket awarded to the winner.

The US Open

Various courses, USA — Organized by the USGA, the US Open is known for its brutally difficult course setups. It is open to any professional or amateur who survives qualifying, making it one of the most grueling tests in the sport.

The Open Championship

Various links courses, UK — Simply called "The Open" by those in the know, this is the oldest major in golf, dating back to 1860. Played on classic links courses like St Andrews and Royal Troon, it is defined by wind, rain, and raw tradition.

The PGA Championship

Various courses, USA — Organized by the PGA of America, this major is known for attracting the deepest field of any major. It has produced some of golf's most dramatic finishes and is held each May.
